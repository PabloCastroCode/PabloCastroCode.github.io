Projects

Stack Wallet

I designed a modular wallet that is MagSafe-compatible, trackable (using NFC tags before AirTags existed, which also require no charging), portable, and lightweight. Our project won 2nd place in our school competition, earning us $300 and the support of a patent lawyer.

P-Trap Sink Modification

Because of different complications with the drainage system in my home's kitchen sink pipe, my family and I were seeing water drain slowly. Unable to identify a cause, I created a solution (a mesh pipe cap for the p-trap) to allow an additional exit for the water. Now we know there was a build up of mold, but even after removing it we have kept my end cap as it's also more durable.

Pool Ladder Stopper

My home's pool ladder kept on scraping the wall because our rubber stoppers would shrink and fall off. So, I 3D printed a different version to act as a more permanent fix.

Spring-powered Butterfly and Heart

I saw a video of rubber-band-powered butterflies flying out of a book. Trying to replicate it, I designed 3D-printed, rubber-band powered spinning hearts. Using what I learned, I traced a monarch butterfly on Fusion and combined it with the same spring mechanism to bring it to life.

Piranha Plant Drain Cleaner

I 3D-Printed a drain cleaner to clean a shower in my house. As I used it, I thought it looked like a piranha plant so I designed a new one that did. I even made the stand a pipe so it looks like it's from the Super Mario games.

Recyclable Spool Container

One of the challenges made by Bambu Lab involved recycling, so I made lids to be able to recycle cardboard filament spools with center diameters of 82mm, 87mm, and 92mm.

Tarot Card Case

My brother needed a card case for some cards he bought so I took some measurements and made him a box that fit perfectly and had designs on it.

Belt Clip

Being a skinny guy means that most of my pants and belts don't fit my waist, so I made this clip/buckle to hold a longer belt in place.

Luggage Tags

One of my friends lost track of his bag on a school competition. Luckily after about two hours of searching for the bag we found it at a restaurant we had eaten at. The owner couldn't contact us because he didn't have our information, since then I have just been 3D-printing luggage tags for people.

3D Printed Products for Hermanos de la Calle

The service organization with which I have been involved the longest is Hermanos de la Calle, where we feed and care for the homeless. Common problems they face involve storms, and cleanliness. Storms lift up their tents during storms as many don't have a place to pitch them (they reside on sidewalks). For that I created carabiners to be able to pitch the tents to the fence, while they get back up on their feet. My second fix involves cleanlier storage for toothbrushes and soap bars. I made these fixes with my 3D Printer.

Business Card

I made business cards which have our information on QR codes and NFC tags. Anytime someone wants to contact our business they can just save our contact on their phone by scanning. It's cheaper, harder to lose, and proffesional.

Desk Organizer

My first 3D-printed project was a universal desk leveler. After several prototypes, I realized that I was solving a problem that could be fixed just by loosening a screw or slipping in a piece of paper, so I scrapped it. Still, the process taught me to enjoy researching, collecting data, and applying it toward solutions.

Leaf Net

Because of expensive landscaping fees, communal discontent, and lack of municipal help, banyan trees and their leaves created a big issue in my neighborhood. So, I decided on making a product that would solve it. I came up with a net that has a hole in the center which when lifts up and acts as a funnel into a garbage bag. This tackled the problem of collection. By adding grass clippings and water I transform them into fertilizer. But this process of disposal is difficult as it requires a lot of space to store the bags while they ferment.

Heart Lithophane

3D printing makes creating gifts easy. After gifting my mom, dad, and brother some heart lithophanes with special pictures, I have been asked by extended family to make them their own lithophanes as they love how they come out.

Cross Bookmark

After my dad gave me books like The Intelligent Investor, One up on Wall Street, and High Returns from Low Risk I started reading more. Leading me to come up with a bookmark in the form of the holy cross.

Arduino Traffic Light Project

My first project with an Arduino was making a Traffic Light. I learned the basics of circuitry and programming.
